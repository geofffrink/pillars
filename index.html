Pillar 03 — Delayed Gratification
The Marshmallow
Experiment
Stanford researchers gave 4–6 year olds a choice: eat one marshmallow now, or wait and get two.

They followed these kids for 40+ years. The ones who waited had better SAT scores, higher educational attainment, lower BMI, and better outcomes across nearly every life measure tracked.

The ability to delay gratification is critical for success.
3
Pillar 03 — Delayed Gratification
What it
Looks Like
  • When my friends were out at bars in my late 20s, I was home learning WordPress.
  • Skip the television. Get the homework done. Learn more. Get better grades.
  • Skip the dessert at the store. Eat healthier at home.
  • Finish the workout. Put in a few more reps. Get stronger.
3
Pillar 03 — Sidebar
Beware
Shiny Objects
Success in nearly every field requires you to ignore the easier path in favor of the harder one.

You will be tempted constantly. New tools, new opportunities, new distractions. The discipline is not in starting — it's in staying.
3
Pillar 03 — Sidebar
What Is Your
Time Worth?
$228
Per hour — Annual income ÷ 12 months ÷ actual working hours
Every hour you spend not working, you're paying that rate to do something else. Social media hits different when you reframe it that way.

But keep your escapes. Without them, you burn out. Just be self-aware about where your hours go.

$820M Net Worth

Bonus — The Seinfeld Strategy
Don't Break
the Chain
Every day you do the work, put a big red X. After a few days you'll have a chain. Keep it growing. Your only job is to not break the chain.
