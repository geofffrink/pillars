Pillar 01 — Niching Down
Master
of One
The world rewards depth over breadth. You cannot be everything to everyone — in your career, your relationships, or your pursuits.

The people who win are the ones who pick a lane, go deep, and become genuinely exceptional at it. Spreading yourself thin is comfortable. Going all-in on one thing is terrifying — and that's exactly why most people never do it.

Pillar 02 — Eliminating Regret
You miss 100% of the shots you don't take.
Yes, it's scary. Taking the shot always is.

But here's the thing — either outcome moves you forward. You go for it and it works, and you surprise yourself. You go for it and it doesn't, and now you have feedback that shapes your next move.

The only result that gives you nothing is not trying at all.

Pillar 02 — Eliminating Regret
Enduring

There's a reason salespeople are the highest-compensated role in any business — they get rejected ten times a day and keep going anyway.

You will not close the sale without the cold call. You will not score the date without asking.

Pillar 03 — Delayed Gratification
The Marshmallow
Experiment
In the 1960s, Stanford psychologist Walter Mischel sat children ages 4–6 in a room with one marshmallow. The deal: eat it now, or wait 15 minutes alone with it and get two.

Simple choice. Profound results. Researchers followed these same kids for over 40 years — and the ones who waited consistently outperformed the ones who didn't. Better SAT scores. Higher educational attainment. Healthier BMI. More stable relationships.

One marshmallow or two predicted life outcomes better than almost anything else they measured.
3
Pillar 03 — Delayed Gratification
What It
Looks Like
When my friends were out at bars in my late 20s, I was home learning WordPress.

Skip the TV tonight — get the work done and learn something new. Skip the junk food — eat better tomorrow. Don't leave the gym early — put in five more reps.

Every small sacrifice compounds. The person you'll be in 10 years is being built by the choices you make right now.

Pillar 03 — Sidebar
Beware
Shiny Objects
Success in nearly every field requires you to ignore the easier path in favor of the harder one.

You will be tempted constantly. New tools, new opportunities, new distractions. The discipline is not in starting — it's in staying.

Additional Anecdotes — The Seinfeld Strategy
Identify It.
Do It Daily.
Don't Stop.
Want to get better at something? Identify it. Commit to 15 minutes a day. No excuses.

Jerry Seinfeld — one of the most successful comedians of all time — was once asked his secret by a young comic. His answer was simple: write better jokes. And the way to write better jokes is to write every day.

Get a wall calendar. A full year on one page. Hang it somewhere you can't ignore.
Additional Anecdotes — The Seinfeld Strategy
Don't Break
the Chain
Every day you do the work — even just 15 minutes — put a red X on that day. A few days in, you have a chain. A few weeks in, you have momentum.

Consistency beats intensity. Your only job is to not break the chain.
